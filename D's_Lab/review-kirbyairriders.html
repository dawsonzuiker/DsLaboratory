Kirby Air Riders

★★★★☆
4.0 / 5

Overview

Kirby Air Riders is a fun and chaotic alternative to Mario Kart World. It leans more into arcade chaos and personality than strict racing, offering a very different experience from traditional kart racers.

Gameplay & Feel

The single player experience is not especially engaging. It feels similar to Smash Bros. Ultimate’s single player mode, which never really clicked for me. Where the game truly shines is in multiplayer. The races are chaotic, surprisingly in depth, and demand constant attention.

The air rider movement takes time to adjust to. It can feel awkward at first, but once it clicks, the gameplay becomes much more enjoyable. It ends up feeling like a proper sequel to the original game, which I loved growing up. There is a strong sense of nostalgia baked into the presentation.

The visuals are bright and colorful, the music is excellent, and the character variety adds a lot of personality. Even when playing online, every match felt different thanks to unique character and kart setups from other players. That variety keeps matches feeling fresh.

While the game is very fun, it does fall short of Mario Kart World in overall polish and consistency. It does not replace it, but it stands well on its own as something different.

Final Thoughts

Kirby Air Riders is a colorful and nostalgic multiplayer focused game that works best with friends. While its single player mode is lacking, the chaotic races, strong presentation, and variety make it an enjoyable arcade style experience.
