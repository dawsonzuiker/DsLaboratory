How I Review Games

My reviews are built around one simple thing: how the game feels when you actually play it. I’m not trying to sound like a critic, and I’m not chasing hype. If it’s fun, it gets credit. If it’s boring, it gets called out.

I focus on the parts that matter most to players. How good the gameplay loop is. Whether the game stays fun after the first hour. If the systems feel rewarding or feel like chores. If the movement and controls are smooth. If the game respects your time.

I also factor in replayability, community longevity, and value for the price. Some games are amazing once. Some games stay in rotation for years. That matters, and that’s what D’s Laboratory is about.

  • Gameplay feel (movement, controls, pacing)
  • Replayability (does it stay fun)
  • Grind and progression (rewarding or annoying)
  • Modes (multiplayer, co-op, side modes)
  • Value (worth your time and money)
